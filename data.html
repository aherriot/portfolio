\r\n\r\nIn the Spring of 2012, I took a class on Fault Tolerant Computing (CSC 454).  For the final project, we were able to explore the fault tolerance of any topic that we wanted, and so Naomi Harrington and Garrett Owen and I choose to look at election systems.\r\n\r\n
\r\n
\r\n\r\nTo compare different electronic voting systems against each other was more challenging than we first expected.  The makers of voting machines do not readily make available the various failure rates of their systems.  However, reading through research and literature of the topic, we were able to create a simulation using good-enough estimates for various faults.  Those these numbers may not be entirely accurate, they are likely to reflect the relative performance of the systems against each other.\r\n
